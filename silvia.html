Nissan S13 Silvia Goes Sideways

Nissan blessed the S13 with their newly developed multi-link rear suspension and at around 2,700 pounds, provided the sort of handling to punch up in competition. All of that sounds like a solid, fun-loving, affordable sporty option for the masses, but the reason the S13 went on to obtain legendary status among the enthusiast circle was due to its drifting heritage. With Japan's drift movement in full swing, the low entry price combined with a very capable SR20DET and performance-minded suspension made the modern S-chassis a no-brainer for those interested in honing their sideways car control. So popular was the S13, that Nissan continued producing the 180SX version until 1998, which overlapped the next generation S-chassis.

  • Production Years: 1988–94 (180SX continued until 1998 in Japan)
  • Japanese Engines: CA18DE, CA18DET, SR20DE, SR20DET
  • Horsepower: 131, 168, 145, 205
  • Torque: 117, 166, 132, 202
  • U.S. Engines: KA24E, KA24DE
  • Horsepower: 140, 155
  • Torque: 152, 160
  • U.S. Pricing: approx. $13,500 – $22,000
  • U.S. Market Competitors: MR2, Miata, Eclipse, Camaro, Mustang

Exterior Overview

Coupes like the S15 are a dying breed. Finished in Silky Snow Pearl (WK0), this 1999 Silvia Spec S has optional Aero side skirts, fog lamps, and 5-spoke Skyline alloys. With 121k miles on the clock, the Silvia is turn-key ready for backroad touge jaunts or your next car show. Only a few cosmetic defects exist, keeping it in above-average shape.

Interior Overview

The interior is simple, durable, and functional. Dual SRS airbags protect occupants, while the dash, center stack, and upholstery remain in good shape after 26 years. Features include Bluetooth Kenwood audio, digital automatic climate control, power windows, locks, mirrors, and keyless entry. Upgraded Autogauge instrumentation monitors oil temperature, pressure, and water temperature (note: pressure gauge non-operable).

Mechanical Overview

Powered by the smooth and reliable SR20DE 2.0L engine, paired with a 5-speed manual gearbox, the Silvia S15 offers peppy performance with easy maintenance. Recent updates include a new 12v battery, alternator, engine air filter, fuel filter, spark plugs, front caster arms, fresh rubber on all corners, and a new alignment. Factory suspension and ABS brakes ensure precise handling and safe stops.
